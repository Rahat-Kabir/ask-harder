The project I'm proudest of is a billing reconciliation service I led at my last company. We were losing roughly 2% of revenue to mismatches between our payment provider's records and our own ledger, and finance was reconciling by hand in spreadsheets. I owned the design and most of the implementation: a nightly job that pulled settlement files from Stripe, matched them against our ledger with a tolerance window, and surfaced unmatched transactions in a review queue. I designed the matching algorithm, built the ingestion pipeline in Python, and worked with finance to define the escalation rules. After three months in production it had recovered about $40k in missed charges and cut the manual reconciliation time from two days a month to under an hour. It also became the template for two other data-matching services other teams built later.
