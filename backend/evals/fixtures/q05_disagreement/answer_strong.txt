On a search feature, a teammate wanted to bring in Elasticsearch and I pushed back, arguing Postgres full-text search would be enough and one less system to operate. The stakes were real: Elasticsearch meant new infrastructure, on-call burden, and sync complexity, but if Postgres couldn't handle our query patterns we'd be rebuilding in six months. Rather than argue in the abstract, we agreed on acceptance criteria first — p95 under 200ms on two million documents with our actual query mix — and each spent a day building a spike against a production-shaped dataset. Postgres held up fine on relevance and latency for everything except fuzzy matching, which we measured as under 3% of queries from the logs. I had actually gone in expecting Postgres to lose on relevance, so part of the exercise changed my own assumptions too. We went with Postgres, and we wrote up the decision and the benchmark numbers as an ADR, including the explicit trigger for revisiting: if fuzzy-match queries passed 10% or the corpus passed twenty million documents, Elasticsearch goes back on the table. The teammate ended up presenting the ADR at our guild meeting. Two years later the trigger still hasn't fired.
