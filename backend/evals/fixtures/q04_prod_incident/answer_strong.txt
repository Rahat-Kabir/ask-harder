The one I learned the most from was a slow-burn memory leak in our order-processing workers. Over about six hours, processing latency crept up until orders started landing in our dead-letter queue — roughly 5% of orders were delayed during the worst hour. I was on call. First I confirmed the blast radius from the queue dashboards, then correlated the latency curve with worker memory in Grafana, which pointed at a leak rather than load. I took a heap snapshot from one of the degraded workers and found an unbounded in-process cache that a recent change had started keying by order ID instead of product ID, so it never got cache hits and never evicted. We mitigated immediately by cycling workers on a schedule, then shipped the real fix the next day: bounding the cache with an LRU and adding the eviction behavior to its tests. The lasting changes were a memory alert on workers at 70% with a runbook, and we added heap snapshot tooling to the base worker image so the next person doesn't have to improvise it during an incident. The postmortem was blameless — the bug had passed review because nothing in CI exercised cache hit rates.
