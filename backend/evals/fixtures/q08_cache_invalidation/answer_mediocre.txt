The simplest approach is a TTL on every cache entry — say sixty seconds — so data is never more than a minute stale. How long you can tolerate depends on the data: product descriptions can be stale for an hour, account balances basically can't. If writes go through your own service, you can also delete or update the cache entry whenever the underlying data changes, which keeps things fresher than TTL alone. The trade-off is that shorter TTLs mean more traffic hitting the slow service again.
